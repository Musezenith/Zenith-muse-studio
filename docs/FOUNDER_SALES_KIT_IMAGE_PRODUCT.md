## 2) Core messaging (ready-to-say)
### Short pitch (15-20s)
“Musezenith turns image generation into a full creative decision workflow: brief to variants, compare side-by-side, mark finals, and export a clean handoff bundle.”

### Medium pitch (45-60s)
“Most teams can generate images, but they still lose time deciding and handing off. Musezenith gives you a structured flow: PromptLab builds the prompt package, generates variants, Archive preserves runs, Compare helps pick winners, Final selection marks approved outputs, and Export creates a shareable artifact. It’s designed for faster campaign iteration with less prompt chaos and clearer review decisions.”

### Problem -> Solution -> Outcome narrative
- Problem:
  - Teams generate many images but lack consistent prompt structure, decision workflow, and traceable handoff.
- Solution:
  - PromptLab + Archive + Compare + Final selection + Export in one workflow.
- Outcome:
  - Faster concept cycles, clearer approvals, reusable run history, and cleaner client/internal handoff.

## 3) Qualification framework (quick fit check)
Use this simple `F I T` framework.

`F`requency:
- Do they run visual concept cycles weekly?

`I`teration pain:
- Do they struggle with prompt consistency, review friction, or repeated revisions?

`T`eam workflow need:
- Do multiple people review/approve outputs and need traceable artifacts?

Strong-fit lead if:
- High frequency + clear iteration pain + multi-stakeholder review process.

## 5) Discovery call script (20-30 min)
### Opening (2 min)
- “Goal today: understand your current image concept workflow, then see if our shipped flow can reduce cycle time and approval friction.”

### Discovery questions (10-12 min)
1. “How often do you run image concept iterations?”
2. “What breaks most often: prompt consistency, output quality, or approval process?”
3. “How do you compare options and decide finals today?”
4. “What artifact do you send to clients/internal stakeholders for review?”
5. “How much rework comes from unclear decisions or missing context?”
6. “What would a successful pilot improve in 2-4 weeks?”

### Qualification wrap (3 min)
- Confirm `F I T`.
- If strong fit: move to demo + pilot frame.

## 6) Live demo script (real app flow, 12-18 min)
### Demo objective
Show brief-to-final workflow with real decision and handoff.

### Steps
1. PromptLab setup:
   - Enter brief.
   - Pick preset/model/variants/seed policy.
2. Generate:
   - Click `Generate Prompt + Images`.
   - Mention run states and retry/cancel.
3. Review package:
   - Tabs: Positive, Negative, Params, QC, JSON, Score.
4. Generated outputs:
   - Show variant grid.
5. Archive:
   - Open saved run.
   - Use search/filter/sort quickly.
6. Compare:
   - Put candidates in Compare Left/Right.
7. Final selection:
   - Mark run final and one or more image finals.
8. Re-run:
   - Trigger `Re-run` from archive item.
9. Export:
   - Open detail drawer -> `Export bundle`.
   - Explain bundle content: prompt/result/metadata/assets refs.

### Demo close line
“This is the complete flow from creative brief to approved candidate with traceable handoff.”

## 8) Objection handling (practical responses)
### “We already use image generators.”
Response:
- “Same here; we focus on the workflow after generation: compare, final decision, and handoff traceability.”

### “What if generation fails?”
Response:
- “You get explicit run states and can retry or cancel safely. Failed runs are still traceable.”

### “What if assets disappear or storage has issues?”
Response:
- “Assets are remote-first with per-asset fallback-inline behavior. Missing assets degrade gracefully instead of breaking the workflow.”

### “How do we share this with clients?”
Response:
- “Use the export bundle from Archive detail; it includes prompt/result/metadata and asset references.”

### “Will this fit our current review process?”
Response:
- “Yes, we start with one real campaign brief and adapt demo/pilot to your existing approval flow.”

## 9) Call checklist (before / during / after)
### Before call
1. Pick one ICP-relevant brief example.
2. Ensure demo environment is running.
3. Prepare one strong and one fallback narrative.
4. Define desired next step (pilot/discovery extension).

### During call
1. Confirm problem and urgency first.
2. Demo only shipped capabilities.
3. Tie each feature to a workflow outcome.
4. Confirm pilot success criteria verbally.

### After call
1. Send recap in under 2 hours.
2. Include agreed pains, mapped workflow, and next step.
3. Share scheduling link and pilot proposal.

“Would it help if I map your current review steps to our shipped flow in a 1-page before/after? That usually makes the go/no-go decision easier.”

## 11) Closing CTA options
Use one clear ask at a time:
1. “Book pilot kickoff.”
2. “Run one real campaign brief together.”
3. “Approve success criteria and owner on your side.”

Closing line:
- “If we can reduce your concept-to-approval cycle in the next 2 weeks, are you open to moving this into your standard workflow?”

## 12) Early pilot success criteria (use in deals)
Minimum success markers:
1. At least 3 runs completed in real workflow.
2. At least 1 compare decision and 1 final selection set.
3. At least 1 export bundle used in actual review.
4. Stakeholder confirms reduced review friction or faster decision time.

## 13) Internal operating notes
- Never promise capabilities not visible in current shipped flow.
- Keep language outcome-first, feature-second.
- Lead with process reliability, not model hype.
- Track objections and update this kit weekly.
